## RSH MENU GUIDELINES - COMPREHENSIVE RULES

### 1. FORMATTING RULES

#### 1.1 Dish/Drink Names
- Font: Calibri (Body), 12pt, Bold
- **Case: DO NOT change capitalization.** Preserve the chef's capitalization exactly as written, whether sentence case, title case, or all caps. Do not flag, suggest, or auto-correct capitalization on dish or drink names — even if a casing scheme appears inconsistent with the SOP. This is a deliberate policy override of the SOP capitalization rule.

#### 1.2 Descriptions
- Font: Calibri (Body), 12pt
- **Case: DO NOT change capitalization.** Preserve the chef's capitalization in descriptions as written. Do not flag, suggest, or auto-correct capitalization (including words like "Australian", "Roasted", "Grilled", "Braised", etc. appearing capitalized). This is a deliberate policy override of the SOP capitalization rule.
- Descriptors separated by commas, forward slashes, or dashes depending on design

**Grammar in dish names:**
- Use "choice of" not "choice" before options: "choice of chicken or vegetables"

#### 1.3 Prices
- Format: Whole numbers only
- NO: Dollar signs ($), cents (.00), "USD"
- Dual prices: Use vertical bar ` | ` to separate (e.g., "11 | 40" for glass/bottle)
- Prix fixe: Use 00.00PP format; treat `PP`/`pp` as a valid per-person price suffix, so values like `50.00PP` and `50.00pp` count as prices.

#### 1.3.1 Missing Price Detection (CRITICAL)
Every dish on a standard (non-prix-fixe) menu MUST have a price. If a dish entry has a name and description but no price, flag it as a suggestion with:
- type: "Missing Price"
- severity: "critical"
- Do NOT auto-correct missing prices in the corrected menu — leave them in SUGGESTIONS only
- A trailing whole number counts as the item price even when there are no allergen codes before it, such as `Short Rib al Carbón, housemade tomatillo sauce, pickled red onion 54`
- For prix fixe menus, individual dishes do NOT need prices (only the overall prix fixe price is required). Do NOT flag missing individual dish prices on prix fixe menus, and recognize an overall price at the top such as `50.00pp` as the required price.
- For embedded set-menu/prix-fixe subsections inside a standard menu (for example, a title like "Quick Lunch Menu $38" followed by "choice of one appetizer & one entrée"), included dishes do NOT need individual prices. If an included dish has a trailing bare item price, flag it as type "Set Menu Item Price" with severity "critical" and do NOT remove the price from CORRECTED MENU. Explicit plus prices such as "+5" or "+ AED 50" are premium/supplement upcharges and are allowed.

#### 1.3.2 Incomplete/Missing Dish Names (CRITICAL)
Every menu entry must have a recognizable dish name. Flag entries that are:
- Just a description without a dish name (e.g., "grilled, served with salsa 24")
- Just a price with no dish name
- Partial or truncated names that appear incomplete

Flag these as a suggestion with:
- type: "Incomplete Dish Name"
- severity: "critical"
- Do NOT auto-correct — leave in SUGGESTIONS only

- Do NOT flag standalone menu instructions such as "choose one", "choice of one",
- "select two", or "pick your entree" as incomplete dish names or missing prices.
- These are selection instructions, not dish entries. Preserve them in CORRECTED MENU.

#### 1.4 Allergens
- Format: Superscript, same line as dish/drink name
- Final allergen clusters should be comma-separated, no spaces, and alphabetical, but deterministic pre-AI checks already normalize recognized trailing clusters before this review.
- Alphabetical order means standard A-to-Z order of the code tokens, NOT the order the codes appear in the allergen legend. For example, "D,G" is already correct; never recommend changing "D,G" to "G,D".
- Case: Always capitalized

#### 1.4.1 Menus With No Allergen Codes (CRITICAL)
- If NO dish line anywhere on the menu carries allergen codes, the menu has no allergen program. Emit ONE suggestion with type "Allergen Code", severity "critical", confidence "medium", menuItem "Entire menu", describing that no dishes carry allergen codes and recommending coding per the allergen key.
- In addition, for dishes whose visible ingredients clearly indicate a major allergen, emit per-dish "Allergen Code" suggestions (severity "normal", confidence "medium") with the specific code(s): S for shellfish (shrimp, lobster, crab), N for nuts, D for dairy (cheese, cream, butter, crema), G for gluten (noodles, pancakes, tempura, wonton).
- Do NOT auto-add these codes to CORRECTED MENU when the menu has no allergen program at all — surface them as suggestions for the chef to confirm.

### 5. DESCRIPTIONS

#### 5.1 Hyphenation Rules

**USE hyphens for compound adjective modifiers (adjective + past participle):**
- sun-dried (tomato)
- house-made
- wood-fired
- dashi-braised (bok choy)
- adobo-marinated
- karaage-style
- Stir-Fried (Wagyu Beef)
- Singaporean-style
- Corn-Fed (Chicken)
- 24-Hour Braised (Short Ribs) - time-based compounds use hyphens

**USE hyphens for compound cooking methods:**
- stir-fried
- pan-seared
- slow-roasted

**USE hyphens for compound flavor modifiers before nouns:**
- miso-guajillo (marinade)
- miso-chipotle (sauce)
- yuzu-chipotle (dressing)
- lime-chili (dressing)

**DO NOT use hyphens for ingredient combinations:**
- peanut guajillo (NOT peanut-guajillo)
- mango chipotle (NOT mango-chipotle)
- lime cilantro (NOT lime-cilantro)
- guajillo macha (NOT guajillo-macha) - Exception: if part of a sauce name like "guajillo–macha sauce"

**CRITICAL — Preserve ingredient separators exactly as written:**
- DO NOT change hyphens to commas or vice versa (e.g., keep "pineapple-habanero sauce" exactly as-is)
- DO NOT split compound words with hyphens unless a specific rule above applies
- Only apply the hyphenation rules above when the pattern clearly matches; when in doubt, preserve the original punctuation
- Every change to ingredient separators or punctuation MUST be flagged as a correction — never silently rewrite

#### 5.2 Redundant Words to AVOID (Flag as Suggestions)

**Food descriptions - remove these:**
- creamy, warm, fresh
- pepper (unless specifying type)
- garnish, mesclun

**Drink descriptions - remove these:**
- juice (just say "lime" not "lime juice")
- puree (just say "mango" not "mango puree")
- syrup (just say "agave" not "agave syrup")
- vodka, tequila, rum, gin, bourbon, whiskey (assume liquid form in cocktail)

#### 5.3 Cheese Rule
Always include "cheese" modifier:
- mozzarella cheese, Manchego cheese (NOT just "mozzarella, manchego")
- Applies to: Mozzarella, Manchego, Oaxaca, Mexican, Goat, Blue, Panela, Cotija, Brie, Pecorino, Parmesan, Cheddar, Swiss, Provolone, etc.
- Note: Manchego should always be capitalized

#### 5.4 Dish Name Consistency
When a dish name includes both protein and preparation method, the preparation/style typically comes first:
- "Miso Salmon" (NOT "Salmon Miso") - the miso preparation modifies the salmon
- "Grilled Octopus" (NOT "Octopus Grilled")
- Exception: When the protein IS the dish name, keep it first: "Salmon, achiote ponzu..."

### 7. ALLERGENS

#### 7.1 Dynamic Allergen Detection

**IMPORTANT**: Each menu document may have its own allergen key/legend. You MUST:
1. First, look for an allergen key or legend in the document (usually at the bottom of the menu)
2. Use the codes and definitions provided in THAT document
3. Apply those specific codes when reviewing menu items

**Common allergen key formats:**
```
C crustaceans | CE celery | D dairy | E egg | F fish | G gluten | L lupin | MO mollusc | MU mustard | N nuts | P peanut | SE sesame | S soya | SU sulphites | V vegetarian | VG vegan
```
or simpler versions like:
```
D dairy | G gluten | N nuts | S shellfish | V vegetarian | VG vegan
```

#### 7.2 Formatting
- Deterministic pre-AI checks already normalize recognized trailing allergen clusters to uppercase, comma-separated, no spaces, and alphabetical order.
- Do NOT re-report an allergen alphabetization/formatting edit that has already been applied.
- Continue to review whether the codes themselves are missing or incorrect.
- Example final format: `Dish Name D,G,N 18`

#### 7.3 Ingredient-to-Allergen Mapping

Use this knowledge to identify missing allergen codes based on the document's allergen key:

**DAIRY / D**
- Milk, cream, butter, cheese, yogurt, ice cream, crème fraîche

**EGG / E**
- Eggs, mayonnaise, aioli, hollandaise, béarnaise, meringue, custard

**FISH / F** (NOT shellfish or crustaceans - do not use S or C for fish!)
- Salmon, tuna, cod, anchovy, branzino, sea bass, seabass, swordfish, hamachi, sake (salmon sashimi), maguro, suzuki, sea bream
- Fish sauce, Worcestershire sauce, bonito flakes, dashi, furikake
- IMPORTANT: Branzino, sea bass, seabass, sea bream, and other whole fish are FISH (F), NOT shellfish (S) or crustaceans (C)

**CRUSTACEANS / C** (shelled creatures with legs)
- Shrimp, prawns, lobster, crab, crawfish, langoustine, kani (crab)
- NOT the same as molluscs!

**MOLLUSC / MO** (soft-bodied, often in shells or no shells)
- Squid, octopus, calamari, clams, mussels, oysters, scallops, snails, abalone, urchin
- IMPORTANT: Calamari and octopus are MOLLUSCS (MO), not crustaceans (C)

**GLUTEN / G**
- Wheat, bread, tortilla (flour), pita, naan, pasta, beer, soy sauce, fried food (breaded), pie crust, roux

**NUTS / N** (tree nuts)
- Almond, brazil nut, cashew, hazelnut, macadamia, pecan, pine nut, pistachio, walnut
- Also: pesto, salsa macha, praline, marzipan
- Note: Write "cashew" not "cashew nut" (redundant)

**PEANUT / P** (separate from tree nuts - CRITICAL distinction!)
- Peanuts, peanut butter, peanut oil, satay sauce
- IMPORTANT: If a dish contains peanuts, it needs BOTH P and N allergens
- Example: "Papaya Pomelo Salad, green mango, peanut, lime chili dressing P,N,VG"
- Peanuts are legumes, not tree nuts, but often listed with both codes for safety

**SESAME / SE**
- Sesame seeds, tahini, hummus, sesame oil

**SOYA / S**
- Soy sauce, tofu, tempeh, edamame, miso

**CELERY / CE**
- Celery, celeriac, celery salt

**MUSTARD / MU**
- Mustard, mustard seeds, mustard powder

**SULPHITES / SU**
- Wine, dried fruits, pickled foods

**LUPIN / L**
- Lupin flour, lupin seeds (common in European baking)

**VEGETARIAN / V** (flag if dish contains)
- Meat, poultry, fish, seafood, gelatin, rennet cheese, lard, chicken/beef broth

**VEGAN / VG** (flag if dish contains)
- Any animal products: meat, fish, dairy, eggs, honey
- IMPORTANT: Ice cream, cream, butter, cheese = DAIRY = NOT VEGAN
- If dish has dairy, use V (vegetarian) NOT VG (vegan)

### 8. RAW/UNDERCOOKED ITEMS (*)

#### 8.1 When to Apply Asterisk

**SEAFOOD**
- Oysters on the Half Shell, Sushi & Sashimi, Ceviche, Crudo, Poke, Tiradito
- Raw tuna, salmon tartare, hamachi, any raw fish preparation
- Chef's Sushi Selection, Sashimi platters
- Dishes with tobiko (fish roe) - often raw
- Fish/prawn gyoza may need * if fish is undercooked
- Crispy Rice Salmon (if salmon tartare on top)
- Sushi rolls with raw fish (Spicy Salmon, Shrimp Roll if raw components)

**MEAT & POULTRY**
- Steak Tartare, Carpaccio, Beef Tataki
- Rare or Medium-Rare Steak, Wagyu filet, Wagyu rib eye (typically served undercooked)
- Duck Breast (Seared, Medium-Rare)

**EGGS**
- Hollandaise & Bearnaise Sauces, Caesar Dressing (Traditional)
- Sunny-Side-Up or Over-Easy Eggs, Fried & Soft-Boiled Eggs, Tiramisu
- Deterministic pre-AI checks already handle clear poached egg and sunny-side-up egg markers when the line has a safe allergen/price suffix; review remaining ambiguous egg preparations.
- Cured egg yolk - needs * (curing does not fully cook)

**OTHER**
- Cheeses Made with Raw Milk
- Meringue (Uncooked or Lightly Cooked)
- Certain Cocktails (e.g., Whiskey Sour with egg white)

#### 8.2 Asterisk Placement Rules (CRITICAL)

Deterministic pre-AI checks already normalize existing raw asterisk placement and add missing markers for the strongest raw-item terms. Continue to review ambiguous raw/undercooked cases. If you add or preserve a marker, the asterisk (*) must be attached to the last word of the dish/description, BEFORE the allergen codes and price.

**Correct placement patterns:**
```
Dish Name, description* ALLERGENS PRICE
Dish Name* ALLERGENS PRICE
```

**Examples:**
- `First Kiss, yellowfin tuna, leche de tigre, ají amarillo, truffle oil, root chips, furikake* 24`
- `Toro Love & Fire, 6oz Australian Wagyu filet mignon, jumbo prawns, guajillo–macha sauce* D,S 85`
- `Angry Zengo, spicy tuna, avocado, lemon, yuzu kosho aioli* E,F,SE 78`
- `Sashimi* F` (for sashimi items, asterisk goes before allergen)
- `Chef's Selection Sushi and Tiradito* F`

**Key rules:**
1. Asterisk goes at END of description, not after dish name
2. Do not leave a space before the asterisk: use `description*`, not `description *`
3. For items with only allergens (no description), asterisk goes before allergens: `Sashimi* F`
4. Sushi/sashimi items need both * AND appropriate fish allergen (F)

#### 8.3 Required Footer
Culinary pages MUST include:
`*CONSUMING RAW OR UNDERCOOKED MEATS, POULTRY, SEAFOOD, SHELLFISH, OR EGGS MAY INCREASE YOUR RISK OF FOODBORNE ILLNESS`
